⠀Your score explained

For a Band 7 in Reading, test takers demonstrate a good understanding of complex texts on a range of topics. They can effectively identify main ideas, supporting details, and the writer’s attitude and purpose. Test takers can generally understand both explicitly stated and implied meanings, though they may occasionally miss subtleties in highly nuanced or complex passages. They can differentiate between key points and less relevant information, and they handle a wide vocabulary, including some academic and topic-specific terms, with occasional difficulty in very specialized language or abstract ideas.




⠀Advice to improve your score

Enhance your reading skills by exploring a wide range of texts, especially those with unfamiliar vocabulary. Focus on understanding the main ideas and relationships between sentences, even if you need to make educated guesses. After reading, look up any words or phrases you didn’t understand to build your vocabulary. Additionally, practice reading materials that require quick information retrieval, like schedules, menus, and announcements. Use the same techniques you’d use in your native language to locate key details swiftly, such as skimming and scanning. This will strengthen your ability to find relevant information efficiently during the exam.
